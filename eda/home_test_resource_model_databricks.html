Introduction to Model

The aim of this model is utilize historical home testing lat flow data and create an efficient resourcing model.

Using Arima forecasting model, forecast is produced which will give us hourly number of tests.

We build our resourcing model based on our hourly forecast and using following assumption:

  • Assumptions made in the model are as follows: * Mvine test are 60% of total home tests Webform based tests are 40% of total home tests

  • for mvine tests 1 tests takes approximately 2 mins to complete

  • for webform tests 1 tests takes 10 mins to complete

  • shifts are on 4 hour basis i.e 8-12, 12-16, 16-20, 20-24.

Resourcing Model

Now we have our forecast. We use two methods to produce final number of workers required per shift:

  • Max tests per 4 hour shift
  • Avg tests per 4 hour shift

In max tests method we look at max test in any 4 hour shift. One shift is for 4 hours and we cant change the number of workers during a shift. We look at the no of home tests forecaster per hour for a 4 hour a shift and use max test out of 4 hours to work out how many workers are required per shift so they can accommodate busy hour.
